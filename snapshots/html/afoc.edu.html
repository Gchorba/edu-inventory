academic

AEWC (Alternative Education and Work Center) - is a high school dropout recovery program. Cousework ranges from basic skills to high school diploma. .

High School Diploma - Flexible schedule for student to complete course at any time of the day - morning, afternoon, and evening.

GED Testing and Preparation - We're an authorized center for GED testing. GED can may be taken in English, Spanish, or French.

ABE (Adult Basic Education) -Refresh your strength in basic language, math and reading skills. A placement test is required prior to enrollment.

AIS (Adult Independent Study)- Earn high school credits at home on coursework and turn in weekly assignments to the instructor.

vocational (cte)

Industrial Career - Prepares students through academic and hands-on training for careers in various skilled trades including automotive mechanics, computer repair, culinary arts, graphic arts, and the construction trade. Provides real-life experience through work contracts with private businesses and government-funded organizations.

Business Education - Provides short-term employment training; skills upgrading; and career preparation.

Health Occupation - Provides opportunities for entry-level positions in health care. With the passage of a required exam, the majority of classes result in state licensure or certification.

Apprenticeship Program - Designed to identify applicants who are interested in becoming state-indentured apprentices in the building and construction trades as well as in barbering and cosmetology.

In the Counseling office

AFOC offers all levels of ESL classes. Lessons focus on daily living skills and effective communication in an English-speaking environment. Placement tests are given to students before enrollment.

ESL Citizenship - Provides students with an understanding of the US naturalization process, American history, and rights and responsibilities of US citizens.
