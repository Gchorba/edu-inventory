Oxford College associate professor of psychology Ken Carter is co-author of a new textbook, Learn Psychology, that creates an interactive learning context through the use of multiple platforms. Students can access course materials not only in print but also via desktop and laptop computers, iPads, smart phones and e-reading devices. "Learn Psychology offers a new approach to the introductory psychology textbook that is both interactive and engaging to read," Carter says.

The Emory community is busily preparing for its 167th Commencement! We invite you to review the schedule of events and make plans to attend the long weekend of festivities in May. The graduation ceremony for college, professional and graduate school students is planned for May 14, with the keynote address by neurosurgeon and humanitarian Benjamin Carson. Held on the Quadrangle, the ceremony offers a unique expression of university values, including leadership, service and academic integrity.

Emory's Oxford College holds its own Commencement ceremony. Oxford College Board of Counselors chair Zoe Hicks will give the keynote at that event on May 12.

A $1.1 million grant from The Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation will expand the William H. Foege Fellowships in Global Health at the Rollins School of Public Health. The new funding will increase the number of fellows each year and help fulfill a program goal of maintaining ties with fellowship alumni, says Deborah McFarland, associate professor in the Hubert Department of Global Health and director of the Foege Fellowship Program.

"This grant will allow us to plan a meeting to bring all of our fellows and alumni together as part of the warranty that we have for the program -- to keep them connected to Emory and us to them," says McFarland.
