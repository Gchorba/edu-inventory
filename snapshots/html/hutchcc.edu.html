Alive!

Springtime on the HCC campus brings a riot of color. The grounds at HCC have been honored with the Best Landscaping Award from the Hutchinson News Readers Choice Poll three times since the award program started. The grounds come alive with blooms, and feature more than 30 different varieties of trees from around the world.

Walking

HCC President Dr. Ed Berger cuts the ribbon on the new Blue Dragon walking trail on campus.

The trail, which winds one mile around the HCC campus, was an initiative from the HCC Wellness committee. The trail has markers at the 1/4, 1/2 and 3/4 mile markers and is available for anyone to utilize throughout the year.

Preparing

With the spring storm season beginning in Kansas, students of the HCC Emergency Dispatch Program are preparing for every contingency. HCC students receive high-tech training on the very equipment they will use in the field after graduation.

The HCC 911-Dispatch Center is the back-up call center for 19 counties in South Central Kansas.
