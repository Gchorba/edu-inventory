  Welcome to the Technical College System of Georgia
  
 

The Technical College System of Georgia is a system of technical education, custom business and industry training, and adult education programs. Our programs use the best available technology and offer access to lifelong learning for all Georgians. The TCSG promises its students a guaranteed education.

Download and view the new 2011-2012 TCSG Fast Facts and College Directory to learn more about the system and its colleges. iPhone and iPad users can also save it in iBooks by clicking the link and tapping the top right of the screen.

 

Go Build Georgia: Great careers don't always begin with a four-year degree and end in a corner office. Welcome to industrial and commercial construction, where the education is hands-on, the work is rewarding and careers are built from the ground up. The TCSG colleges are proud partners in the Go Build Georgia campaign. You can learn more on the Go Build Georgia website or watch the WSB-TV special "Rebuilding Georgia".

 

The TCSG is now on the semester system. Semesters offer TCSG students additional instructional time, in-depth coverage of course content and should ease transfers to other colleges and universities. TCSGsemester411.com has all the information needed to learn about semesters at the TCSG colleges.


Adult Education Grant Notice. Adult Education providers are invited to apply for federal funding to deliver adult education services in jurisdictions in Georgia. Interested agencies may obtain the Request for Application (RFA) from the Technical College System of Georgia (TCSG), Office of Adult Education (OAE) website at: http://literacy.myweb.uga.edu/rfa.shtml or call (404) 679-1625 for additional information.

Students: lost most of your income due to job loss or underemployment and need help with your mortgage?
The Georgia Department of Community Affairs can help with a statewide mortgage payment assistance program to prevent foreclosures. The Georgia DCA HomeSafe Georgia program will provide "bridge" mortgage assistance for up to 18 months.


TCSG Launches CollegeThatWorks.com: The TCSG and Georgia Department of Labor have partnered in a new public awareness program that provides unemployed Georgia males with information about how the TCSG colleges can help them to refocus on new careers and at little cost. CollegeThatWorks.com features videos, a Back to Work Guide, Quick Facts brochure, and much more.

 

GA Education TV partners with the TCSG and OneGeorgia Authority to provide grants for local schools to use the U.S. Education TV platform. The requirements for this grant are that the school must be in an OneGeorgia Authority eligible county and must have an agreement with the local TCSG college to consult and provide support for this initiative. For more information click here.
