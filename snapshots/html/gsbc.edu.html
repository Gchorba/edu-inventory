Chapel Messages

Published on April 8th, 2012

At Golden State the highlight of the day is chapel – a time to be spiritually refreshed and challenged. Take a minute and listen to our chapel message archive.



From Fisherman’s Wharf in San Francisco to the beautiful beaches of Santa Cruz, the Bay Area is full of many exciting things to do and see. There are many opportunities to get away from school and fellowship with other students. Kimberly Baltazar (Milpitas, CA)
Pastor Ray (Pastor of Hopewell Baptist Church, Napa, CA) teaches Personal Evangelism. In this class, he has given me a true heart for souls. He has given me a desire to go soulwinning and to reach as many souls as possible. Tabitha Ross (Jasonville, IN)
The choir [of NVBC] is always great and the special music is phenomenal. The atmosphere…really encourages us younger college students to stay faithful in the ministry. Aaron Waddell (Freeport, PA)
On Saturdays, we have [the] opportunity to be in the bus ministry and [attend] the bus rally with Bro. Boruff. We have an awesome time. It’s a chance to get to know other students that you might not see during the week. Nathan Garcia (Nampa, ID)
I came here not knowing anybody, and Bro. Trieber was one of the first to come up to me and introduce himself. He has such a personal relationship with each of us. Zachary Church (Milwaukie, OR)
On Wednesday, Pastor Trieber comes to chapel. It’s one of the highlights of our week! His spirit is infectious. He makes us want to serve the Lord just as he does. Lauren Flood (Milpitas, CA)
The teachers are incredible men of God that [teach] us so much from the Bible. They don’t only teach the Bible, but it’s also evident that they live it. Andrew Brown (Madison, AL)
It’s so great to have leaders in your life that are so accessible. You can just talk to them about life and how school is going. The teachers actually care about you as a person. Matt Leonard (Jackson, MI)
Not only am I learning information, but I’m learning how to apply that in the classroom with different teaching techniques. Alysa Ruiz (Lakewood, CA)
They force you to learn, to grow, and to stretch your limits. John O'Malley (Kings Mountain, NC)
Pastor Trieber’s testimony is something every student can admire and emulate. Zachary Langston (Santa Clara, CA)
I can’t imagine what my life would have been like if I had gone anywhere else. Jeff Harrell (Almo, KY)
