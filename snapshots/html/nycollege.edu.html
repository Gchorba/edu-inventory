Welcome

New York College of Health Professions has been a pioneer in Holistic Health and Wellness for 30 years. It is the only private institutionally accredited college in this field in the metropolitan area.  The College mission is to provide education and community service in these areas.

The campus is conveniently located in Syosset, Long Island, with 3 additional locations in New York City in Manhattan on the upper west side at the world famous Riverside Church (120th Street near Columbia University and Barnard College), downtown on Houston Street in the University Settlement in the Soho/Chinatown neighborhood and in a new midtown site at the NY Open Center on East 30th Street.  New York College also owns a facility in China where students and friends can visit.

The College has one of the finest and largest Holistic Health Clinics which provides affordable treatments to the public in Massage Therapy, Acupuncture, Reflexology and Herbal Medicine.

New York College is the only private institutionally accredited school in this field in the tri-state area.  It has institutional accreditation by the New York State Board of Regents and the Commissioner of Education*, a nationally recognized accrediting agency, and accreditation from the Accreditation Commission for Acupuncture and Oriental Medicine (ACAOM) for its Masters programs.
*located at 89 Washington Avenue, Albany, NY 12234 (518) 474-3852.

Academics

New York College offers accredited undergraduate and graduate Degree Programs, as well as Continuing Education and Certifications in health, science, self improvement and topics of interest.

Massage Therapy Programs
The School of Massage Therapy is a great entry point into the field of holistic health.  The accredited Associate degree in Massage Therapy can be completed in less than 2 years and credits can be transferred for higher degrees. New York College also offers a Bachelor degree in Advanced Asian Bodywork.
Learn more about the massage therapy school >>

Oriental Medicine
The Graduate School of Oriental Medicine offers an ACAOM accredited program in Oriental Medicine (the study of Acupuncture and Chinese Herbology).  Graduates receive a Bachelor of Professional Studies/Master of Science degree.
Learn more about the oriental medicine school >>

Acupuncture Program
The Graduate School also offers an ACAOM accredited Acupuncture program, the first such program approved in New York State. Upon completion students receive a combined Bachelor of Professional Studies/Master of Science degree.
Learn more about the acupuncture school >>
