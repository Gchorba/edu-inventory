  • The Year In Ideas: Venture capital for undergraduates »

    Free funding
    for student “start-ups”

    Our idea: give students stipends to explore whatever interests them. Work on a political campaign? Join an archeological dig? Write web content for a fashion designer? Sure. With resources like Wheaton Fellows and Davis International Fellows programs, the Filene Center  provides the advice and funds for more than 200 internships. It’s like having angel investors behind you even before you graduate. And the only thing they expect in return is your enlightenment.

  • The Year In Ideas: Literary analysis, 140 characters at a time »

    The sense and sensibility
    of literature in the age of technology

    We are media omnivores. We love books and blog posts, feature films and tweets, journal articles and graphic novels. In some classes, like English 290 (which is all about literary and culture theory) we use it all. The class reads the book, watches the movie, dives into long discussions and dissects ideas in the Twittersphere. It’s the liberal arts, next generation.

  • The Year In Ideas: Career services at the Filene Center »

    Learning for life
    For now and later

    Ok, it’s been around for a while, but every year it seems like a better and better idea: not just a career center, not just a place to plan your academic or professional future, not just a global internship bazaar–but all of those things at once. This is where you go for internships, fieldwork, summer and part-time jobs, career workshops and networking events with alumni, plus advising in a range of fields (architecture, communications, law, management, premed and other health professions). A four-year, all-season, holistic approach to learning and life.
