What's Happening at Hallmark

The Hallmark students work towards this all year, the Phase IV Year-End Portfolio Assignment. 27 images, 27 prints to be produced and presented as a Final Portfolio in just 10 weeks.

Week Thirty-Four:

  • Video Photography using DSLR Cameras
  • Photographers and their Web Sites
  • Portrait and Tabletop Commercial Studio Photography
  • Photojournalism — On Location Interviewing Skills
  • Welcome Back October 2012 Graduates
  • Special Guest Lecturer Rick Souders

Many of this week's classes are continuations for the June Class of 2012. For example, in Video they spend their time working on their year-end group video projects. This year's class are using their Canon 5D Mark II cameras to capture video footage (this is the same camera they are issued for the entire program).  They work in teams on all aspects of their project, starting with writing, directing, post-production, editing and finally, publishing.

Web Site Design is set aside for students to continue work on their personal/business web sites, which is a mandatory part of their Final Year-end Portfolio assignment.  As part of their Career Business Plans, students identified how they will incorportate their Web site as part of their business.  Almost everyone will use it as a publically viewable portfolio, but some will also have private client galleries and E-commerce on their sites to streamline client communication.

Portrait and Commercial Studios remain busy as  our June 2012 graduating students continue to shoot for this portfolio. The role of the Instructor has changed in the studios.  They act as advisors and mentors more than before and are on-hand to provide assistance and guidance as needed.

Location Studio takes them on location to one of the local newspaper offices, The Gazette and they are instructed on what to expect from an interview, and will even do some role-playing, in their Interviewing Skills class.

They get back into the swing of things by reviewing some assignments they turned in before the break, as well as new lectures in Portrait and Commercial Photography and Photojournalism.

Both classes get to enjoy a full-day presentation by Guest Lecturer Rick Souders. A Denver-based food photographer, Rick will lecture on the ins and outs of food photography and share his award-winning imagery with the class, delving into this very interesting and profitable niche of our industry. 


Dennis O'Clair photo

Invading the Office of the President, Artist-in-Residence Gregory Heisler and Instructor David Turner work with Hallmark students to demonstrate the photographing of an executive in his environment. Being able to mix various light sources, both natural window light as well as artificial light, is a crucial part of creating a successful executive portrait.
