  • Porter-Gaud Boys Tennis Wins State Title Over Hilton Head Christian

    Porter-Gaud’s boys tennis team is back in the saddle again after one of the Cyclones’ longest state title dry spells in two decades.

    Yes, two whole seasons.

    For the 13th time in 18 seasons, the Cyclones (14-2) hoisted the state tennis title Friday afternoon in Columbia after outmaneuvering upstart Hilton Head Christian (8-4) in doubles to claim a 5-4 victory in the SCISA Class AAA state final.

  • Rev. Brian McGreevy '74 Receives the Buell Cup

    The George B. Buell Distinguished Alumnus Award recognizes a graduate who has demonstrated dedicated service and contribution to the school. The honoree may have graduated from any of the four institutions historically connected to what is today “Porter-Gaud School”: Porter-Military Academy, The Gaud School, The Watt School or Porter-Gaud School. The award is named for Col. George B. Buell, Class of 1917, who exemplified the ideals of heritage and tradition.

  • Girls Soccer Having a Banner Year

    First year varsity player, freshman Ansley Thompson broke the seal on this epic Cyclone season with the first, and only, goal of the game to decide the scrimmage versus Beaufort High School. After hanging around the .500 mark for the last few years, the Porter-Gaud Girls’ Soccer team was cautiously optimistic about this early success; however, it ignited a streak of games in which P-G scored 80 goals to their opponents’ 23. This team, compiled of an ensemble of players, young and old, veterans and rookies, proved themselves early with major victories over conference teams such as Hilton Head Christian and Hilton Head Prep as well as larger public schools such as Beaufort High School who they defeated in regular season play 3-2. Head Coach Daniel Jordan and his Assistant Coach Patrick Martin said that they knew the girls had potential from the start, but had no idea how far this team would go.

  • Chemistry Students Titrating Acids and Bases

    Honors Chemistry students practiced their quantitative analysis skills as they performed acid/base titrations in the lab this week. They first standardized their sodium hydroxide titrant using an organic acid. Next, they found the concentration of acid in a commercially available sample of white vinegar by titrating the vinegar with the standardized base. Highlights of the lab included using the highly sensitive analytical balances down in the Advanced Placement stock room and achieving the characteristic faint pink color expected when one reaches the endpoint using phenolphthalein.
