career areas.

CTC School to Work Banquet 05/03/2012

The Career Technology Center of Lackawanna County recently held its annual “School-to-Work” Banquet honoring local employers and students that participate in Cooperative Education.  The Cooperative Education program at the Career Technology Center of Lackawanna County allows outstanding students to explore an actual work experience in their selected field.  This is a one-year opportunity for seniors who have been recommended by their career and technical education instructor and sending school. The work experience is designed to introduce students to the fundamentals of the actual employment setting and apply the techniques, skills, and academics gained throughout their educational experience. If you would like to obtain more information on the Cooperative Education program at the Career Technology Center of Lackawanna County contact Earl Bishop at 570-346-8471.

CTC Students Reach Gold04/26/2012

 

 

 

The following students from the Career Technology Center of Lackawanna County are competing in the SkillsUSA National Championships. The competition takes place at the H. Roe Varle Hall, the Kansas City Convention Center and the Municipal Auditorium in Kansas City, Missouri, on June 26. The students earned the right to compete when they won first place at the Pennsylvania SkillsUSA Championships on April 20, 2012 in Hershey, Pennsylvania. They were accompanied by the SkillsUSA Advisor, Jenine J, Ikeler, MS.

Randy Jarosh, Firefighting Competition, Mid Valley High School

Natalie Wasko, Medical Assisting Competition, West Scranton High School

Nelson Salcedo, Telecommunications Cabling Competition, Scranton High School

 

SkillsUSA is a partnership of students, teachers and industry representatives; working together to ensure America has a skilled workforce. It helps each student to excel. Skills USA is an applied method of preparing America’s High performance workers in public career and technical programs. II provides quality education for students in leadership, teamwork, citizenship and character development. It builds and reinforces self-confidence, work attitudes and communication skills. The associations emphasize total quality at work, high ethical standards, superior work skills, life-long education and pride in the dignity of work. SkillsUSA also promotes understanding of the free enterprise system and involvement in community services actives.
