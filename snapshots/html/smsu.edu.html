SMSU Commencement May 5

Posted 04.27.2012

A total of 647 Southwest Minnesota State University students will receive either undergraduate or master’s degrees during the 44th commencement exercises at 10 a.m. on Saturday, May 5 in the R/A Facility on campus.

Dr. Thomas Jackson, the Vice President for Student Affairs at the University of Louisville and a 1985 alumnus, will give the commencement address.

The Minnesota State Colleges and Universities (MnSCU) system will be represented by Louise Sundin, a member of the MnSCU Board of Trustees. She is executive vice president of the Minneapolis Regional Labor Federation, AFL-CIO.

Professor of Education Dr. Sharon Kabes will be honored during the ceremony as the 2012 recipient of the Cowan Award, named after SMSU Psychology Professor Cathy Cowan, who was killed in a car accident in December 2001.

Theme Selected for 2012 Homecoming

Posted 04.26.2012

The theme of Homecoming 2012 will be “Mustangs for Life: Southwest Through the Ages.”

Homecoming will be the week of Oct. 1-6. 2012.

The theme was announced on the SMSU Facebook page on Wednesday, April 25.

The goal of the theme is to engage the campus community and alumni by capturing the many eras of Southwest. It is being released early so that it gives students a chance to raid their parents’ closets and grandparents’ basements for retro clothing and memorabilia.

Jackson is 2012 Commencement Speaker

Posted 04.09.2012

The first keynote address heard by this year’s senior class was by Dr. Thomas Jackson, back in 2008. The final keynote address they’ll hear — during commencement on May 5 — will be from the same man.

Jackson gave the convocation address to the Class of 2012 when they were incoming freshmen, back on Aug. 22, 2008. He’ll address them again during commencement May 5, which begins at 10 a.m. in the R/A Facility on campus.

Jackson is the Vice President for Student Affairs at the University of Louisville. He is a 1985 SMSU alumnus, former Student Association president and a big supporter of the university.

“I remember the majesty of the cornfields, and that relationships matter,” he said. “I came to Minnesota from Seattle, Wash., and I was moved by how interconnected people were — how families were interconnected, how people found ways to not only work collectively, but also how they relied on each other for their own success or livelihoods, in distinct ways.”
