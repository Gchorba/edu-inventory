Top Stories

During the summer months, Hiwassee College will be offering classes that are open to the public. Dr. Beth R. Scruggs, Vice President and Academic Dean said, “We are excited to be able to once again offer summer classes to traditional aged students who wish to get an early start on their college courses. Many students take advantage of the opportunity to complete core courses before the regular Fall or Spring term. Non-traditional students, including military personnel and working adults, are also encouraged to apply.”

The May Mini-Term at Hiwassee College runs from May 8 through May 25. Course offerings include: American Literature, Information Literacy, Communication, College Algebra, General Chemistry and Developmental Math.

Left: Dr. Beth R. Scruggs, Hiwassee College Vice-President and Academic Dean

Top Stories

On Saturday, April 28, over 1,500 students representing more than 25 East Tennessee high school concert bands will be participating in the Tennessee Secondary Schools Band Director Association (TSSBDA). The bands annually compete for a rating of Superior, Excellent or Good. This the sixth year that the event will be held on the Hiwassee College campus. Keith Trout, Assistant Director of the Sequoyah Band said, “We are very happy to partner with Hiwassee College and pleased about this opportunity to showcase the talented young musicians in the area.”

Left: Linda Trout, Sequoyah High School Band Director, holds a plaque representing the TSSBDA Concert Festival Award.

On Saturday, May 5, the Dental Hygiene Class of Hiwassee College will take part in a pinning ceremony on campus. This event is a symbolic welcoming as new dental hygienists enter the profession. Dr. Shelley Diviney, Program Director, said, “We are very proud of these students and their accomplishments. They have successfully completed all of the requirements of the dental hygiene program.”

Top Stories

During the week of April 15, Hiwassee college students were invited to participate in a series of workshops dealing with “professional etiquette”. Lisa Rouvelas, coordinator for the events said: “We were very pleased with the participation and enthusiasm of the students. They learned how to project both a positive image and confidence using eye contact and appropriate dress.” Students engaged in a series of “roll play” exercises to practice shaking hands and introducing one another. Both students and faculty members expressed support for expanding the series. Ms. Rouvelas is working to explore the possibility of integrating future programs into the calendar of activities offered to students. For information on the etiquette workshops, Lisa can be reached by calling 423-836-4854 or by Email at rouvelli@hiwassee.edu

Top Stories

Following the final home basketball game of the season on February 14, Bethany Allen was honored as a member of the Hiwassee College 1,000 Point Club. She is the first basketball player at Hiwassee College to join the elite club since the school became a four-year program in 2008. Allen, a junior from Polk County, TN, amassed over 1,000 career points in less than three seasons on the basketball court.

This has been her most productive season on the college level. Allen is leading the team in points, rebounds, and blocks. During the 2011-2012 season, she is averaging a “double-double” (16 points per game as well as 11 rebounds).

Hiwassee College women’s basketball coach, Matt Cravens says of Allen, “It has been a pleasure to be around her for the last two years. She is a joy to coach and strives every day to better herself and her teammates.” Coach Cravens went on to say, “Bethany’s time at Hiwassee is not over yet. We still have a couple more games left in this season, and we plan on her being a huge part of our success in the 2012-13 season, so we continue to expect big things from her.”

The Lady Tigers wrap up their 2011-12 regular season with road games at Tennessee Temple University followed by the College of Coastal Georgia. After that, it is on to the NCCAA Division I, Mid-East Regional Tournament in Chattanooga, TN.
