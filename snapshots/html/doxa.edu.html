Doxa, the Graduate School for you We live in a world saturated with emotional problems. Today more than ever it's required of men and women of leadership to prepare in order to meet this demand.

Doxa's parent institution (DIUBCS) has over 25 years of experience training leaders in the area of counseling and psychology. The testimony of these graduates and the experience of those years have become the pillars to the founding of this graduate school, in order to offer sound professional programs sustained by christian ethics.
Empowered to help Counseling as a profession is indeed a calling more then a job. Only those who dare to be significant in the life of others will finish these programs. On the whole, our society in general is suffering and it will take you as a caring professional to make the difference.

Doxa's programs will enable you to be that person!
New Opportunities Our 4 programs (Mental Health, School Counseling, Marriage and Family Therapy and Psychology Doctor) will place you ahead in life with a very competitive edge. The Mental Health program will open up doors in all kinds of clinical environments (Agencies, Hospitals, Private Practice, etc.) The Doctoral Program will train you to become an excellent testing professional providing you with opportunities in the fields of industry, commerce, forensics, etc.

From the President's desk

Welcome to Doxa International University, home of the Graduate School of Psychology. If your interest is to train in Counseling and Clinical Psychology with an accent on Christian Ethics, do not take a step further you have arrived to the institution which will fulfill your dream. Our program is unique in that it takes the highly scientific training you'll find in most schools in America offering similar programs, but committed to a training mission that is faithful to both the scientific-professional discipline of clinical psychology and to our distinctive Christian identity. We refer to this as “integration across the curriculum”, and you will find that we take seriously the relationship between Christianity and psychology, and that we offer seamless integration that occurs in each core course and elective. Our academic community seeks to contribute to the ongoing development of Counseling and Clinical Psychology from a Christian perspective. We pursue this passion through our training, scholarship and service activities. We offer training in counseling and clinical psychology, and we'll seek accreditation by the American Psychological Association, while simultaneously integrating faith and learning throughout the curriculum. The intent of our training is to produce graduates whose career primarily involves psychological practice. Although the faculty represents a variety of clinical orientations, an emerging emphasis in empirically supported treatments is present throughout the curriculum.

Dr. Osvaldo O. Marino President
