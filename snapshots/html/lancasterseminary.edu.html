The Inauguration of Dr. Carol E. Lytch

On Saturday, April 28, 2012 Dr. Carol E. Lytch was inaugurated as the eleventh president of Lancaster Theological Seminary. The event, held at Barshinger Center on the campus of Franklin & Marshall College, was attended by members of the Lancaster community, delegates from theological schools, universities, and churches, staff, faculty, students, trustees, friends, and family.

The charge to the new president was presented by the Rev. Dr. Daniel O. Aleshire, Executive Director of the Association of Theological Schools. Dr. Aleshire's charge, while stating the challenges, clearly expressed the many opportunities that theological schools have to serve those desiring a theological education. President Lytch's inaugural address highlighted the many strengths and accomplishments that have occurred during Lancaster Theological Seminary's 187 years of serving the Church.

"The Seminary is still dedicated primarily to educating ministers for congregations, but it is also more than that. It is education for Christian ministry in many contexts: chaplaincies in hospitals and military settings, counseling centers, nonprofits, advocacy organizations, and teaching."

President Lytch's vision for the theological schools and specifically Lancaster Theological Seminary, was articulated well and spoke of the bright, prosperous, and challenging future for both the seminary as well as the church.

"The seminary is a servant of the church, that is, the church as it is now and the church as it will be strengthened. The future church will be more global, more affirming of distinct racial and ethnic identities, more open and affirming, more electronic, and more socially-networked."

Dr. Lytch & Dr. Carey on Radio Smart Talk

On the heels of the exciting Inauguration weekend, WITF (89.5 / 93.3 FM) will welcome President Lytch as a guest on Radio Smart Talk this Monday, May 7, 9am - 10am. Join the 1825 Society

As a way to honor the Seminary's past and to safeguard its future the 1825 Society was formed. The name is based upon the fact that Lancaster Theological Seminary was founded in 1825. Since the Seminary's founding it has faithfully prepared leaders for the church and society for 187 years.

Would you like to become part of the Seminary's future? If so we invite you to support the Seminary by becoming a full member in the 1825 Society for the current fund year with gifts to the Annual Fund totaling $1,825 through June 30, 2012. Your gifts will help to support:

  • Relevant and innovative programs of formation for ministry leaders
  • Seminary education that is affordable for all
  • Excellence in technology and resources for education and ministry
