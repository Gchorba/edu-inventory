Wilson Community College receives USI Leadership Award at energy conference

Thirteen Wilson Community College students attended the 9th Annual Sustainable Energy Conference on April 19, 2012. Held at the McKimmon Conference and Training Center at North Carolina State University, the conference drew over a 1000 participants.

Sponsored by the North Carolina Energy Office this year's conference theme was "Sustainability: Optional No Longer". Student attendees participated in sessions on wind and solar energy, clean transportation and clean power including micro-grids. Students also had the opportunity to network with vendors and sponsors in the exhibit hall and attend a ribbon cutting for a new electric vehicle charging station at the McKimmon Center.

Wilson Community College was also recognized at the conference with an award from the State Energy Office for its efforts in energy efficiency. The Utility Savings Initiative (USI) Leadership Award was presented to Wilson Community College for reducing its energy use by 30%. Wilson Community College is one of five community colleges in the state's 58 community college system to meet or exceed the 30% energy reduction and to receive the Utility Savings Initiative Leadership Award.

Unlike other state agencies, including the university system, community colleges are not required to meet mandatory energy reductions required by legislation, which requires that the energy consumption per gross square foot for all state buildings be reduced 20% by 2010 and 30% by 2015 based on energy consumption for the 2002-2003 fiscal year. Even though this particular requirement does not apply to community colleges, Wilson Community College has taken action to reduce energy consumption in an effort to be more sustainable. By virtue of the energy reductions Wilson Community College has implemented over the past several years the college has been able to save Wilson County approximately $577,000 in utility costs since 2003.

Veterans Retraining Assistance Program (VRAP)

VRAP is a new training program for unemployed veterans between the ages of 35 - 60 who are not currently receiving VA educational benefits. The program has a limited number of participates and offers up to $1473.00 per month in benefits beginning July 1, 2012. For the program details & application please visit the following website: http://gibill.va.gov/benefits/other_programs/vrap_email_signup.html

Literary Awards Ceremony held at Wilson Community College

Hezekiah Clemmons, presents a reading from his selection at the 2011-2012 Literary Magazine Awards Ceremony held on Friday, April 20, 2012, in the DelMastro Auditorium at Wilson Community College.

150 students participated in this year's contest. Zelda Lockhart, award winning author of Fifth Born, and the 2010 Piedmont Laureate for Literature in North Carolina, selected the winners for the 2011-2012 Literary Magazine Contest for The Wilson Literary Review. This year's winners are:

  • Cover Art - 1st Place -Rachel White; 2nd Place - Morgan Horner; 3rd Place - Carol Hagler  
  • Literary Winners - 1st Place -Idalia Arellano; 2nd Place - Wesley Roberson; 3rd Place - Deanne Walston
  • Honorable Mentions - Kelsey Hopple; Tequida Parrish; Robert Combs; William Hill; Ortencia Mitchell; Constance Smith; Joshua Moreira

Copies of the magazine, The Wilson Literary Review, are available by contacting the Wilson Community College Foundation Office at 252-246-1293.

Science Adventure Camp

A week long summer camp for rising 6th, 7th, and 8th graders.

The camp will take place on the Wilson Community College campus with trips to the Wilson area Farmers Market, Toisnot Park and the Bridgestone America's Wildlife Habitat Preserve. This camp is intended to encourage greater interest in science and science careers among middle school students through educational and fun hands-on learning activities. Students will be exposed to a variety of hands-on activities and field experiences within the following areas: chemistry, ecology, environmental sciences, renewable energy, recycling, composting, meteorology and weather.

Camp Information:

Camp is Monday, July 9, 2012 - Thursday, July 12, 2012, 9:00 a.m. - 3:00 p.m. and it is $150.00 for the week, lunches will be provided.

All registrations are due by May 15, 2012. Only 10 students will be accepted in the camp. Accepted students will be notified by June 1, 2012. The first 10 registrations will be taken.
