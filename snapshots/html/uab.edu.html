UAB Professor Louise Chow elected to National Academy of Science Louise Chow, Ph.D., professor in the Department of Biochemistry and Molecular Genetics, was elected a member of the National Academy of Science for her excellence as one of today’s pre-eminent leaders in the study of the human papillomavirus, the virus responsible for cervical cancer.

Membership in the NAS is one of the highest honors given to a scientist or engineer in the United States. 

Chow, elected along with 84 new members and 20 other foreign associates, is the second ever elected from UAB.

Breakthroughs

Steroids increase chance of infection in kids with arthritis Children with juvenile idiopathic arthritis have higher rates of bacterial infection requiring hospitalization, and use of high-dose steroids significantly increases that risk, according to UAB research.
 
In findings published in Arthritis & Rheumatism, researchers show that steroid use led to increased risk, while other commonly used medications — methotrexate and tumor necrosis factor inhibitors — did not increase infection risk in children with JIA.

New weight-loss study: take it off, keep it off “As difficult as it is to lose weight, weight-loss maintenance is inherently more challenging," says Gareth Dutton, Ph.D., associate professor of preventive medicine. Anybody who has ever attempted to lose weight can attest to the difficulty.

For more information on the ImWeL study or to take part, call 975-7108 or email IMWEL@dopm.uab.edu.
