Shalom and Welcome to Beth El Yeshiva

    Why attend Beth El Yeshiva?
     
  • To receive a quality Messianic Jewish education
     
  • To prepare for professional Messianic Jewish ministry
     
  • To have your Messianic ministry certified and recognized
     
  • To secure a Messianic Certificate, Associate, Bachelor, Master or Doctoral degree (not required)
     
  • You can do it all through online contact, and at over 50% off average tuition.


    How do I get admitted to Beth El Yeshiva?
     
  • It's easy. First, browse the website by using the menu at the top of the website to get your questions answered. 
     
  • Second, fill out the admission application online and submit the application and application fee. Once we receive your application, you will receive an e-mail notification that we have your application on file.
     
  • Third, wait until you receive your official admission E-mail from us. We will officially accept anyone who is 18 or older, and your permanent file wil be created in our office.
     
  • Fourth, your official admission E-mail from us will include information and merchant links that you will need to place course orders and give you permission and instructions that will allow you  begin to enroll in any course or courses you wish.
     
  • That's it, you're officially a student in the Yeshiva!

    How do I function as a student while enrolled in the Yeshiva?
     
  • First, simply order courses individually or by Semester. Courses will be sent to you by e-mail as a Microsoft Word attachment. (Note: textbooks are usually handled separately according to instructions.)
     
  • Second, work at your own pace. All courses have suggested completion dates, but you can always contact the office to request an extension of time to complete course work.
     
  • Third, once you complete a course, e-mail it directly  back to the course instructor. The exam will then be graded by the course instructor.
     
  • Fourth, once the course work is graded, the Academic Dean will send you an official transcript detailing the total of all your course work grades. It's that easy
