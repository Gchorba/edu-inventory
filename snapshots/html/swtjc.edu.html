SWTJC, Sul Ross presidents sign new memorandum of cooperation
Officials from Sul Ross State University - Rio Grande College and Southwest Texas Junior College were on hand Monday, April 23, for the official signing of a new memorandum of cooperation between the two institutions.

Sul Ross President Dr. Ricardo Maestas and SWTJC President Dr. Ismael Sosa signed the new agreement during a ceremony held on the SWTJC Uvalde campus. VIDEO

Former SWTJC dean will speak at SWTJC commencement ceremony
Dr. Robert Aguero, former dean of instruction at Southwest Texas Junior College, will be the keynote speaker at the 65th Annual SWTJC Commencement Ceremony slated May 12 in Uvalde.

A native of Camp Wood and a graduate of SWTJC, Dr. Aguero began his professional career as a high school teacher and coach at Nueces Canyon High School in Barksdale. He went on to become a college faculty member, assistant to the president and dean of instruction at SWTJC from 1982-1991.

"It is always great to see someone from the area achieve great things and Robert has had a distinguished career in higher education for the past 30 years," said Dr. Ismael Sosa, Jr., president of SWTJC. "We are proud to call Robert one of our own and we look forward to having him with us for our 2012 commencement ceremony."

SWTJC earns coordinating board's best financial rating for 2012
Southwest Texas Junior College is one of 20 community college districts receiving the Texas Higher Education Coordinating Board's best financial condition rating for 2012.

SWTJC Associate Dean of Finance Anne Tarski presented the coordinating board's 2012 findings during the regular monthly meeting of the SWTJC Board of Trustees held April 19 in Uvalde.

"Of the 50 community college districts in the state, only 20 received the coordinating board's highest ranking of satisfactory and we are pleased to be one of them," Tarski said.

Dr. Hector Gonzales named president-elect at SWTJC
Dr. Hector Gonzales is the president-elect at Southwest Texas Junior College. The SWTJC Board of Trustees voted to select Gonzales during its regular meeting on Thursday, March 22, in Uvalde.

A 1988 graduate of Uvalde High School, Gonzales earned his Bachelors Degree in business administration and accounting from Angelo State University in 1992. He went on to earn is MBA from Sul Ross State University in 1996 and received his CPA licensure in 1997.

On Feb. 22 of this year, Gonzales successfully defended his dissertation before a three-member panel at University of the Incarnate Word in San Antonio. He will officially be awarded his Ph.D. in Education, with a concentration in organizational leadership, during UIW commencement ceremonies this May.

Gonzales was hired at SWTJC in 1999 as director of accounting and controller. In 2003 he was named dean of instructional services and chief financial officer.

"Hector has done an outstanding job for us in a variety of capacities and I applaud the board's decision to name him president-elect," current president Dr. Ismael Sosa, Jr., said following the meeting. "Hector is committed to this institution and region. I am confident he will do a great job leading SWTJC for many years to come."

SWTJC instructor Bob Zaiglin honored by The Wildlife Society
Bob Zaiglin, department chair of the wildlife management program at SWTJC, was recently awarded The Wildlife Society's prestigious deer management career achievement award.

The award was presented to Zaiglin during the 35th Annual Southeast Deer Group Meeting in Sandestin, Florida.

Dr. Steve Demaris, professor at Mississippi State University and chairman of the Southeast Deer Group, recognized Zaiglin for "his oustanding contributions to the understanding of white-tailed deer ecology and management in the southeastern United States."

Member states in the society's southeast section include Missouri, Texas, Delaware, Maryland and West Virginia. Last year, Zaiglin was awarded the 2011 outstanding achievement award ty the Texas Chapter of The Wildlife Society.

The wildlife management program at Southwest Texas Junior College currently has 122 freshmen and sophomores enrolled in the only two-year program of its kind in the state.

SWTJC Cowboys claim league basketball championship in San Antonio
The SWTJC Cowboys are the 2012 champions of the South Texas Club Sports League.

"It was a perfect end to a great season," coach Richard Flores said. "Congratulations to all our players for their hard work this spring and last fall."

SWTJC claimed the title on Sunday, March 11, with a 100-95 win over Victoria College. Trailing 49-48 at the half, the Cowboys rallied for the win behind top scorers Jeremiah King with 29 points and John Register with 24.

SWTJC's John Register (pictured on the right) was named tournament most valuable player following the Cowboys win over Victoria College in the tournament title game.
