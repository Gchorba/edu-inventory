  • Students place second and third in National History Day state competition

    Rachel Schwitzgebel '14 placed second in the National History Day state competition, held on Saturday, April 28, for her historical paper, “The Revolution Against Galenistic Beliefs and Theories: William Harvey’s Circulatory System Research.” She will advance to compete in the national contest at the University of Maryland from June 11-14. Mary Stuart Fountain '14 placed third in the state contest.

  • Visual and Performing Art students recognized; Ira David Wood III delivers keynote

    Saint Mary's School Visual and Performing Art students were recognized for their achievements on Wednesday, May 2, 2012 at the Celebration of the Arts award ceremony held in Pittman Auditorium. Several students were also honored for their recent accomplishments in local and regional competitions. The audience welcomed Theatre in the Park's Executive and Artistic Director Ira David Wood III to deliver the keynote speech for the occasion.

  • Saint Mary's School names 13th Head of School

    The Board of Trustees of Saint Mary’s School is pleased to announce the appointment of Dr. Monica M. Gillespie as the 13th Head of School in Saint Mary’s 170 year history. Monica brings to Saint Mary’s nine years of head of school experience, a passion for educating girls, deep appreciation for the Episcopal heritage of Saint Mary’s School, and a distinguished background in education. She will be both an exemplary leader and a remarkable role model for our students.

  • Vestry tradition continues with officer induction chapel

    The new 2012-2013 vestry officers received their official blessing and affirmation during the annual vestry officer induction chapel service, held May 1, 2012, in the historic Saint Mary’s Chapel. The vestry, as in all Episcopal parishes, is the lay leadership committee that works with the parish’s clergy on the temporal affairs of the parish.

  • 2012-13 student leaders inducted

    Congratulations to the Student Government Association (SGA) members, Marshals and Class Officers recently elected or appointed to serve as student leaders in the 2012-13 school year. In a quaint Chapel ceremony on Thursday, April 26, these new leaders were officially inducted into their upcoming roles.

  • SUMMER SPOTLIGHT: Leadership Camp

    We believe every girl has the potential to be a leader. Even with plenty of growing up left to do, girls can and do make a difference in their various communities – school, family, neighborhood, church, teams and clubs. Girls will be challenged to think about what leadership is and what they, as unique individuals, have to offer. We will help shape their leadership by giving them practical, essential communication skills. Spread the word to family, friends and neighbors about this new camp opportunity that will be offered June 11-15 for girls rising 6th-8th grade. Read more for additional details and registration instructions.

  • World Languages Department inducts new members

    On Thursday, April 19, during a special ceremony in Pittman Auditorium, the World Languages Department inducted its newest memebers into the Saint Mary's School French, Latin and Spanish Honor Societies. In addition to meeting the academic requirements and satisfying other criteria as set forth in the Saint Mary's handbook, a student must demonstrate good character, citizenship and personal motivation to be considered for induction.

  • Thirteen students inducted into National Art Honor Society

    On Wednesday, April 18, thirteen students were inducted into the Saint Mary's National Art Honor Society (NAHS). Several members of the community stopped by Kenan Library to view the inductees artwork and celebrate their accomplishment. NAHS was established by the National Art Education Association in 1978 to promote standards of excellence in visual arts education.

  • Black Curtain Coffeehouse offers student playwrights platform to shine

    The Black Curtain Coffeehouse was introduced in 2006 by the Visual and Performing Arts Department as one of its two annual drama productions. Five years later, the coffeehouse is the hottest ticket on campus during its annual weekend run, playing to standing room only houses in Chan-Poyner Hall. The last three coffeehouse playbills have featured original plays written by Saint Mary’s students.

  • Chair of Visual and Performing Arts wins second place for artwork

    Teresa Assenzo, Chair of Visual and Performing Arts at Saint Mary's School, won second place for her piece titled "Palimpsest" in Unfettered, a special exhibit at the Visual Art Exchange, a prominent gallery in downtown Raleigh.
