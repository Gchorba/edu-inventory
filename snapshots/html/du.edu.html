Leaders Come to Campus

Former Secretaries of State Condoleezza Rice and Madeleine Albright are just two of the dignitaries visiting the University as part of the Presidential Debate Event Series, an array of forums, talks and seminars that will engage the public in the issues of this fall’s election.

Farms in the City

Urban Farming, an international studies course available this summer, digs into the political and economic forces driving the local food movement. Students will explore how city farms from Denver to Kenya to Cuba affect health, the environment and other global issues.

  • More about the Urban Farming course
  • See all summer quarter courses
 
 
 

PIONEERS SET THE PACE FOR A NEW ERA

University of Denver athletes are celebrating new records, conference accolades and top rankings for the 2011-2012 season. This year marks a new start for the Pioneers, as 10 teams leave the Sunbelt Conference to compete in the Western Athletic Conference this fall.

  • Pioneer Athletics
  • Catch the last games of the season
 
 
 

Photos from Afar

Our Study Abroad Photo Contest highlights students' best pictures from their work abroad. Outside the classroom, DU students learned about turmoil in Greece; Japanese and Swahili wedding traditions; a Senegalese family's values; Colombian gold mining; and much more.

  • See a slideshow of the winning photos
  • Learn about our study abroad program
 
 
 

A DEGREE OF POWER

Erik Myhren (MA '03) traded a career in advertising for one connecting Denver elementary school kids to academic and extracurricular opportunities. Winner of DU's 2012 Ammi Hyde Award for Graduate Achievement, Myhren credits DU Professor Nick Cutforth with recognizing his gift for working with underserved populations.
