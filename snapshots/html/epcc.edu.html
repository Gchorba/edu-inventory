EPCC to Extend Offer to Presidential Finalist

After having received the PSAC's recommendations and input, heard information on reference checks from the search firm, heard information on his background, received feedback from the Open Forums, and publicly heard information from the delegation that visited South Texas College, in a unanimous vote, El Paso Community College’s (EPCC) Board of Trustees have passed a motion to extend an offer of employment to William Serrata, Ph.D. as its new President in a special Board meeting on April 25, 2012. The offer is conditioned upon the Board of Trustees entering into an employment contract with Dr. Serrata.

New Requirement Before Registration

Meningitis Vaccine Must Be Administered at Least 10 Days Before Registering for Classes

 

Bacterial Meningitis Vaccination is required for all entering students under age 30. The term entering student includes new students to EPCC, transfer students from other colleges or universities, and returning EPCC students that have taken the most recent long semester off from college.

EPCC Will Graduate Over 2,000

El Paso Community College will confer 2,078 degrees and certificates during its spring commencement ceremony at 6:00 p.m., on Friday, May 11, 2012 at the Don Haskins Center. The commencement address will be delivered by El Paso native, Major General Dana J.H. Pittard, Commanding General at Fort Bliss.
