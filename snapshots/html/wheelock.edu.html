What Makes Wheelock Different

  • 1
  • 2
  • 3
  • 4
  • 5
  • 6

  • A Tradition of Service

    Wheelock College students and alumni have been improving the lives of children and families since the College was founded in 1888 in Boston by education pioneer Lucy Wheelock. Watch our story—and then share yours!

  • Commitment to Your Future

    Wheelock's Center for Career and Professional Development works closely with students—as well as alumni—to help them attain their goals, whether those goals are employment or graduate school.

  • Innovative Academic Programs

    Wheelock continues to invest in new, innovative programs—such as our Political Science and Global Studies major launching in September 2012—that provide our students with a wider range of job opportunities and career paths.

  • Community Engagement

    Wheelock’s impact on students, education quality, and child/family well-being far outstrips our physical size. Our initiatives include a high school arts outreach program and a partnership that is revitalizing a community center in one of Boston's most underserved neighborhoods.

  • Field Experience = Industry Experience

    Our students do their field work in schools, hospitals, community agencies, and other diverse and inclusive settings near our Boston campus, learning how to work effectively with all of America's children and families.

  • Talented Faculty Mentors

    Undergraduates may join with their professors to participate in closely mentored faculty research projects, learning to design and implement research projects that result in published papers and presentations.
