    Start Training Now for a Career in Heating and Cooling!

    When deciding where to go to school, there are so many schools, and so many programs.  So why Northwestern Tech? We have been doing this for over 30 years. We have experienced instructors. You will learn by doing. We offer field-focused training. Flexible class times. Employers in the field turn to us when hiring.

    • Right Training

      Field Focused Training

      All of the courses you take at Northwestern Tech are field-related courses that prepare you to get to work.  You are not required to take unrelated courses to earn your certificate.  Our program can be completed in 10 months, and you could be working in the field in less than a year.

    • Right Future

      Employers Turn to Us When Hiring

      Instead of placing help wanted ads when hiring, many employers in the field call Northwestern Tech. because our graduates are well-prepared, and ready to go to work.

    • Right Career

      Career Opportunities

      Employment of heating, air-conditioning, and refrigeration mechanics and installers is projected to increase 28 percent during the 2008-18 decade, much faster than the average for all occupations.

    • About Us
      Flexible class times.

      At Northwestern Tech., we know how busy you are.  We offer morning, afternoon, and evening classes so that you can work around your work schedule or pick the kids up from school, and still be able to attend classes.

    • Benefits
      Learn by Doing

      Reading textbooks and listening to lectures will give you the basic information.  However, at Northwestern Tech., we believe that it is important that you have the opportunity to apply what you’ve learned through hands-on training.  That’s why all of our courses incorporate hands-on lab projects on actual HVAC/R and property maintenance equipment.

    • Why Northwestern Tech?
      Experienced Instructors

      Our instructors teach what they do.  Our faculty consists of  HVAC/R and property maintenance professionals and/or Licensed Mechanical Contractors who currently work in the field everyday and they bring this experience to the classroom.
