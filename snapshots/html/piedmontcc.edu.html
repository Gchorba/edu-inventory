Campus events are scheduled through the Student Activities Coordinator and may include club fundraisers, community awareness events, student government association sponsored events and student activities council sponsored events. The purpose of the student activities council is to ensure that all PCC students receive many opportunities to participate in campus life. A minimum of 2 campus wide activities are scheduled each month and notifications are sent via student email, posted on the activities board in the student center on the Person County campus and on the digital signage located across campus.

Check out the following opportunities to obtain financial aid at Piedmont Community College: Federal Pell Grant, North Carolina Community College Grant Program, North Carolina Student Incentive Grants (NCSIG), Piedmont Community College Foundation Scholarships, Golden L.E.A.F. Scholars Program, North Carolina Education Lottery Scholarship (ESL), Academic Competitiveness Grant (ACG), Targeted Assistance Programs.

Piedmont Community College offers a wide variety of curriculum programs designed to prepare students for the workforce or transfer to a four year college or university. The College is accredited by the Commission on Colleges of the Southern Association of Colleges and Schools to award associate degrees, diplomas and certificates. Students can chose programs that lead to an Associate in Applied Science (A.A.S.), Associate in Arts (A.A.), Associate in Fine Arts (A.F.A.), Associate in General Education (A.G.E.) or Associate in Science (A.S.) degrees.
