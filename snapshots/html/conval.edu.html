Dr. Malcolm Smith to address ConVal High School students, faculty and district parents

Dr. Malcolm Smith, who was instrumental in the development of HB1523, the State Law addressing bullying, will speak to the high school student body and faculty during the school day on April 16th and will follow with an address to district parents and staff  beginning at 6:30pm in the Lucy Hurlin Theatre.  All are welcome at the evening event.

 

Dr. Smith has been caring for and about families for over 30 years.  He is a former member of the renowned Menninger Foundation where he also developed The Peaceful Intervention and Realistic Parenting programs that have been implemented nationally.  Additionally, he has responded to over 200 cases of youth violence in schools and communities throughout the United States and Canada.

 

Currently, as Family Life and policy Specialist at the UNH Cooperative Extension Service, he designs and implements parenting support and training programs.   Recently, Malcolm has written a curriculum on understanding bullying and has conducted workshops across the state aimed at keeping people safe.

 

This event is sponsored by ToAD, a CVHS student group fousing on tolerance, acceptance and diversity and is one of a week long series of events supporting their mission to “Keep ConVal Kind”.

 

For further information, please contact George Kitz in the ConVal Guidance Department (924-4120).

New School Board Chairman Recognizes Outstanding Service

The initial Chairman's Recognition Award highlighted the dedication and service of Jo-Ann Hopkins (Dublin Consolidated School), Marian Alese (SAU 1 Business Office) and Jan Farrington (ConVal High School). All great people, all champions for children. The district is lucky to have folks of this caliber representing ConVal.

Susan Brewer also received a recognition plaque from Chairman Estey for her fast, life saving action recently at South Meadow School. Thank you Susan!

ConVal Special Olympics Team Brings Home Medals

ConVal Special Olympics sent three teams participate in the state basketball tournament in Portsmouth this weekend.  The ConVal teams won 2 gold medals and 1 bronze.  Our teams had a great time and performed very well. Join us in congratulating these hard-working athletes!

ConVal's Production of 'Wit' Receives Honors

The cast and crew of ConVal's drama club won the "Best Production" award at the New England Drama Festival this weekend. They were one of only two NH schools chosen to go to represent our state in the festival. 

Additionally, Cleo Flemming received the Stuart Award for her acting and Ben Carne received the Sarah P. Bunkley Technical Theatre award for his lighting design.  Cleo also received an All-Star award for her acting.

Mission, Vision,
and Values
Mission Statement
The ConVal Regional School District, in partnership with its member communities, will inspire all learners to achieve academically, contribute to the global community, and thrive as independent and productive citizens.
Vision Statement
As a New Hampshire “Follow the Child” district, the ConVal Regional School District will create and cultivate these conditions in the lives of our students:
- Sense of belonging
- Access to heroes and caring role models 
- Sense of accomplishment 
- Fun and excitement 
- Curiosity and creativity
- The spirit of adventure 
- Leadership and responsibility 
- Confidence to set goals and take action to reach those goals
Values Statement
As a Professional Learning Community, we will implement our mission and support our vision by demonstrating:
- A focus on student learning 
- Collaborative teamwork 
- Personal accountability 
- Trust and respect 
- Job embedded professional development
