JMU honors its graduates

It is a time for celebration — the Class of 2012 have taken their place as JMU alumni. Dr. Linwood H. Rose, who is stepping down as president of the university June 30, conferred degrees to approximately 3,567 undergraduate students and 558 graduate students while presiding at his final commencement exercises as JMU's fifth president. Addressing the graduates, Rose said, "As they say, 'all good things, come to an end.' I cannot imagine anyone I would rather 'go out with' than you, the Class of 2012!" As president, Rose has guided JMU to national prominence in higher education. Congratulations to all of our graduating students. Video coverage archive.

Dukes stay connected

The Alumni Arts Courtyard at the entrance of the Forbes Center for the Performing Arts is an expression of a primary goal of the JMU Alumni Association—extending the Madison Experience. The association donated $500,000 to support the Forbes Center to help Dukes stay connected to the university through the high-quality, student-oriented events at the new performing arts center. Thanks, alumni!

Re-birth Day

Connor Gwin ('12), who graduated on May 5 with a degree in religion, has special reason to be thankful for achieving this goal. In 2009, working out with friends, Gwin decided to run some laps. "I made it around about one and a half times and then my heart stopped," said Gwin. In the next few minutes, UREC student staff led by Keala Mason ('10) had to rely on their ability to put training into action to give Gwin a chance to survive.

Job seeking?

Recent college graduates make ideal candidates for the federal workforce—their skill sets are current, they're motivated to work hard to get promoted, and they're innovative and open-minded, says Jeremy J. Ray ('99). He should know. Ray works for an agency within the Department of Homeland Security, and he's encouraging JMU graduates to apply for federal jobs.
