The Future of Urban Transportation: Get Them Out of the Car and on the Bus

There is bus service in Houghton and Hancock, but it's not much used, and parking on or near campus is a knotty problem. With a Ford College Community Challenge grant, two Enterprises at Michigan Tech are analyzing the current public transit situation. They plan to develop a more efficient and sustainable model system.

Getting people back on their feet

Michigan Tech students have developed two inexpensive, functional prostheses—an artificial knee and an artificial foot—especially for India's poor. After traveling to the subcontinent, they are optimistic that their innovations will make a difference.

Enterprising High School Students Showcase Accomplishments in Detroit

Michigan Tech's High School Enterprise program gives high school students a taste of what life is like as a working scientist, technologist or engineer. Teams of high school students partner with business and industry to solve real-world technological challenges. They are showcasing their work in Detroit this week, at a showcase sponsored by General Motors and the University.

Generations of Discovery:
the Campaign for Michigan Tech

Contributions to Michigan Tech help fund many important areas, none more so than our students. They receive financial assistance in the form of scholarships and fellowships, often thanks to alumni and friends of Michigan Tech. Generations of Discovery: the Campaign for Michigan Tech is targeting support for students to ensure they can continue their studies here. All the campaign priorities are on the website.
