Alumnus Ken Wheeler to Address 2012 Graduates

The Rev. Ken Wheeler, a member of the class of 1982, will address the 2012 graduating classes of Trinity Lutheran Seminary and Bexley Hall Seminary during commencement at 2 p.m. on Saturday, May 19. Pastor Wheeler is the pastor of Cross Lutheran Church in Milwaukee, Wisconsin, and served 18 years as an assistant to the bishop of the Greater Milwaukee Synod of the ELCA.

The day will begin with Eucharist at 10 a.m. in the Gloria Dei Worship Center. The Rev. Dr. Henry J. Langknecht, associate professor of Homiletics and Christian Communications, will preach. Graduates will begin their procession from Trinity to Capital University's Mees Auditorium at 1:45 p.m.
 

Summer Term 2012

Summer Term 2012 will offer an array of courses, including a number of music courses,  historical and theological study of alternative religions in America, instruction in family systems, and a travel immersion course in Appalachia. Students can register for one-week or three-week courses for credit or audit. The Summer Term begins June 4. You may register up until Thursday, May 31, on a space-available basis.

Beyond the Seminary Walls

Sabbatical Offers Time for Reading, Writing, and Reflection

The purpose of the sabbatical program is to strengthen the seminary by providing increased opportunity for ongoing growth of the faculty in professional, intellectual, and spiritual dimensions.

-Trinity Lutheran Seminary Faculty Handbook

"A sabbatical is an amazing chance to be away, but in a really fruitful way," said the Rev. Dr. Lisa Dahill, associate professor of worship and Christian spirituality.

Dr. Dahill has been away from campus this year on a sabbatical that has taken her from one end of the country to the other, and even to Norway and Canada. She has penned monastic-inspired poetry, hiked in the Cascade Mountains and prayed along a frozen labyrinth carved in snow. She has contemplated the spirituality of Dietrich Bonhoeffer and read countless books and journal articles about sustainable living and its meaning for our worship and prayer life.
