News Feed

  • Preston Receives 2011-12 Faculty Recognition Award

    Wednesday, May 2, 2012

    Dr. Margaret Preston, associate professor and chair of the History Department, is the recipient of the Augustana Student Association (ASA) 2011-2012 Faculty Recognition Award. The award recognizes outstanding teaching and contributions to the Augustana community by a faculty member.

  • Business Club Students to Attend Meeting With Warren Buffett

    Wednesday, May 2, 2012

    Thanks to a donation from members of  the Sioux Falls business community, student members of the Augustana Business Club will have a chance to participate in a Q&A with legendary investor Warren Buffett. The students will be attending Berkshire Hathaway's Annual Shareholder's Meeting this weekend in Omaha, Neb.

  • Three Inducted Into French Honor Society

    Tuesday, May 1, 2012

    Augustana students Carly Blankenfeld, Madison Hults and Elisabeth Jorde have been inducted into the Mu Alpha chapter of Pi Delta Phi, the National French Honor Society.

Event Calendar

  • May 6: Spring Choral Concert Salutes American Music

    May 6, 2012

    The Augustana Choir, Angelus and The Collegiate Chorale join forces for their Spring Choral Concert, "A Salute to American Music," at 3 p.m. on Sunday, May 6, in Our Savior's Lutheran Church. The concert will feature a number of works from American composers.

  • May 8: Concerto-Aria Concert Features Orchestra and Band

    May 8, 2012

    The annual Concerto-Aria Concert will feature performances by the four student winners of the Augustana Concerto-Aria Competition. The concert will begin at 7:30 p.m. on Tuesday, May 8, at the Washington Pavilion’s Mary W. Sommervold Hall.

  • May 10: College/Community Band to Premier Commissioned Work

    May 10, 2012

    The Augustana College/Community Band, under the direction of Dr. Paul Schilf, presents its last concert of the season at 7:30 p.m. on Thursday, May 10, in the Elmen Center. The concert will feature the world premiere of "Irish Fanfare" by Quincy C. Hilliard, a work commissioned by the Band in loving memory of Erik Ferguson, a former member of the Band and euphonium player.
