Risk

Read Ecclesiastes 9:1 through 12:14

Life offers great opportunities. These opportunities cannot be experienced without a step of faith. Where there is opportunity, there is risk. This makes us wonder, “What if it doesn’t turn out right? What if something bad happens?”

Fear is the robber of good outcomes. It has stolen more reward, more advancement, and more success than any other emotion. To experience a good outcome there has to be a moment of release. You have to let go of something that you have in order to receive what you do not yet have. Yes, there is the risk that what you have might be lost, but there is also the great hope that it will become more than you ever dreamed.

Ship your grain across the sea; after many days you may receive a return (Ecclesiastes 11:1).

Many do not reap the benefits of risk, because they faint during the period of waiting for the good outcome.

The time between the risk and the return can be the most trying, and that may be where you are right now. If you will endure the period of not knowing, you have the promise that you will see the return.

Prayer Suggestion: Lord, help my unbelief!

Quicklook: Ecclesiastes 11:1–6
