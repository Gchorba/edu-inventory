Dwight Tech Chair, Basil Kolani, Curated TEDxNYED on April 28
On Saturday, April 28, educators from throughout the region gathered at the TEDxNYED Conference at the American Museum of the Moving Image in Astoria, Queens. In the true tradition of TED, they spent the day learning about the latest innovations in the field of education and technology, swapped ideas with colleagues, and forged new alliances and collaborations.

The fact that the conference happened at all was, in no small part, due to the efforts of Dwight’s very own Basil Kolani, Director of Information Services and Technology Department Chair. TEDxNYED marked the third conference Mr. Kolani has helped organize under license from TED, the popular non-profit organization with the acronym for Technology, Entertainment and Design. Since 1984, TED conferences and the TED.com website have showcased a wide range of presentations about these areas and how they intersect.

“I’m a huge fan of TED, and I really believe in the mission articulated in its tagline ‘Ideas Worth Sharing,’ ” said Mr. Kolani. “So in 2009, when TED started allowing individuals to set up their own TED conferences, we were among the first to apply for an event centered around education.”

To Mr. Kolani, TEDxNYED is a natural outgrowth of the Dwight mission of finding and nurturing the “spark of genius” within every child. As a teacher, Mr. Kolani is constantly innovating both in terms of leveraging technology to enable improved instruction of a wide range of subjects, as well as instructing students about how they can properly leverage technology in their own personal educational journey. His class on social media was the subject of a New York Times profile in 2011, and more recently he was quoted extensively in Scholastic Administrator’s article “The Evolution of Textbooks.”

Apply Today to the Dwight Global Leaders Academy!
The Dwight Global Leaders Academy is a wonderful opportunity for students in grades 9-11 to explore leadership and service. It is a two-week, hands-on leadership program held each summer at the Dwight International School on beautiful Vancouver Island, British Columbia. Designed for students with a passion for leadership and service, our Academy provides a unique opportunity for young people to learn key leadership skills. Through workshops, activities, team-building exercises, and excursions to rain forests, diverse ecosystems, and historic sites, students will be pushed to step outside of their comfort zones and reach their leadership potential. The program will be held from July 27 - August 10, and applications are being accepted now on the Dwight Global Leaders website.

If you want to see how your child can benefit from this experience, read the article here about graduate Nielsen Dias and his recent recognition by congress with two congressional medals for his volunteer work at various charitable and social services organizations.

Applicants who wish to be considered for scholarships must submit a complete application by May 10. Notification of acceptance will be sent to successful applicants by May 20. Dwight Students Distinguish Themselves on the National Latin Exam
The National Latin exam can be taken by students the world over. Students from all fifty states participated this year, as did some from thirteen foreign countries, totaling over 148,000 participants. The exams cover grammar and reading comprehension, as well as Roman history and culture.
 
Each year, qualifying Dwight students take the exam and we want to recognize the achievement of two students:
 
Mikaela Belenkov '15, who received a certificate of merit for Introduction to Latin
 
Daniil Frants '17, who received a gold medal, certificate of honorable merit, summa cum laude, for Latin I
 
Congratulations to Mikaela and Daniil from Elaine Natalicchi, Esther Schott -- and everyone at Dwight!
