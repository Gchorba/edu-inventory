The Lutheran Seminary Program in the Southwest (LSPS) le da la bienvenida! (welcomes you!).  We are located deep in the heart of Texas where Austin politics, music bands, film festivals, and U.T. football mix to form a unique culture!   In this diverse environment we seek to prepare pastors for ministry in a rapidly changing and pluralistic world that is the mission field of the emerging church of the 21st century.  

LSPS is known for its unique cultural and linguistic accent that is her trademark in theological education.  It does this through a Theological Education for Emerging Ministries Program which we call TEEM. Through this program students develop skills and sensitivity to serve the church in the midst of ambiguity, transition and diversity. They are prepared to address the changing mood and face of our country.   Its particular area of emphasis includes the following: ethnic specific ministry, inner city and rural ministry, renewal of congregations in decline and innovative mission starts. All this is done by following the candidacy requirements of the Evangelical Lutheran Church in America (ELCA) in the formation of students who are prepared and qualified to serve as valued ordained rostered leaders of the ELCA

LSPS Facts

The Lutheran Seminary in the Southwest (LSPS) was started in 1975. Its many achievments include:

  • Graduated over 140 M. Div. candidates
  • Served 180 M. Div. students for part of their theological training
  • Now has graduates serving in 25 states and four countries (USA, Mexico, Chile and Sierra Leon)
  • Graduated 19 TEEM (Theological Education for Emerging Ministries) students who are serving in seven states.
  • Served students from the USA, Colombia, Guatemala, Argentina, El Salvador, Puerto Rico, Mexico, Chile, Nicaragua, The Philippines, Indonesia, Korea and Taiwan.
  •  Has as Faculty the only two Ph.D. Latinos of Mexican heritage in the ELCA.
  • Utilizes the talent and experience of theologians from across the country.
  • Has a donor support base of over 5,000 individuals and 240 congregations in 21 states.
  • Offers continuing education opportunities.
