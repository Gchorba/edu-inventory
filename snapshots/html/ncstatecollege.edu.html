Selections from the Goodman Collection Tue, May 1st
Eisenhower Student Union and Bromfield Information Commons

The Goodman Collection, dating back to the 1940's, first arrive at The Ohio State University Mansfield as a donation from the late Marion and Wynn Goodman of Mansfield. The collection includes works of notable artists such as Pablo Picasso, Jean Miro, Wassily Kandinsky, david A. Siquieros, Triniad Osorio, Masao Iwama, and Pedro Friedeberg. The OSU-M/NCSC Arts & Lectures Committee is pleased to sponsor this event. The collection will be displayed in the Eisenhower Student Union and the Bromfield Information Commons throughout the month of May.

TAG Event - Jessica Pettitt Tue, May 8th, @12:00pm
Eisenhower Student Union

Join us for this very special TAG event. In order to really make change, a person must realize what they are oblivious to and come to understand how this lack of consciousness guides his or her actions and assumptions. We will break down behaviors, actions, assumptions, and stereotypes that limit our organizations, friends, and most importantly, ourselves. Learn the difference between Diversity and Social Justice, and be motivated and inspired to become participants in your own change.

latinos in HD: Reflections on the Acculturation of American Television Mon, May 14th, @12:10pm
Ovalwood Hall, room 100

Theresa Rojas and Christopher Gonzalez, Ph.D. students in the department of English at The Ohio State University, will discuss critical approaches to Latino popular culture in television. Broadly defined, the televisual perspective includes serial drama, comedy, animation, and advertising by, for, and about Latinos. Their 40-minute talk will be followed by a 20-minute open Q&A where they will field questions about their presentation, their work as literature and multimedia scholars and graduate mentors, and other areas of interest. The presentation is sponsored by the Arts & Lectures Committee.

Following the formal presentation, presenters invite students and others to meet them in the Cyber Cafe in Ovalwood Hall
