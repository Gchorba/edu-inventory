Upcoming Events at the Hartford Seminary

Wednesday, May 9th 2012

A Decade of Change in American Congregations: What it Says about Congregational Vitality

This workshop looks at the reality of congregational vitality at the beginning of the 21 century through an interplay of three lenses.  One is the 10 year trend data from the Faith Communities Today 2000 and 2010 national surveys of American congregations.  A second is the wisdom of the current literature on congregational renewal.  The third and most critical is the experience of the participants’ own congregations.

Faculty In The News

Professor Ingrid Mattson will receive an honorary Doctor of Law degree from Trinity College in Hartford, CT, at commencement exercises on May 20. She will be awarded the degree for her service to Islamic communities worldwide, her distinguished scholarship, and her commitment to fostering social progress in the Islamic world and cooperation between that world and other cultures. Announcement
 
The release of Professor Miriam Therese Winter’s new CD, "Loving You, A celebration of All Creation," and her songwriting is recognized in this article.  Also, Professor Winter has written a reflection on her service in Ethiopia during a famine in 1985.
 
A Festchrift is being published in honor of Wayne G. Rollins, Adjunct Professor of Biblical Studies. The volume of writings, titled "Psychological Hermeneutics for Biblical Themes and Texts," will be published in June by T and T Clark International (order here). The Festchrift honors Dr. Rollins' inauguration in 1991 of a research section on "Psychology and Biblical Studies" at the Society of Biblical Literature. A formal presentation of the Festchrift will take place during the 2012 meeting of the International Society of Biblical Literature in Amsterdam, The Netherlands, in July. Dr. Rollins will present on "Psychology and Biblical Studies in 2012: Retrospect and Prospect" at the meeting. Excerpt (which describes events leading to the start of the new SBL section on Psychology and Biblical Studies)
 
Kazunori Hamamoto, a doctoral student at the Theology School of Doshisha University. Kyoto, Japan, is a Visiting Scholar at Hartford Seminary this semester, studying Islamic law. He met students at a reception Sunday evening April 1 and several faculty. He is pictured here with Ibrahim Long, a student, and Professor Ingrid Mattson.   Photo  Photo
 
Professor Yehezkel Landau has published a new essay, "Jewish-Muslim Relations in the 21st Century," on the State of Formation website. Read article. Professor Landau also has published an essay explaining his journey from Israel to Hartford Seminary a decade ago. Read reflection.
 
Professor Uriah Kim has published a new article, "Preaching David and Saul from Multicultural Contexts," in the Living Pulpit magazine. The magazine is offering a free trial through June so if you register on the magazine website, then you can read Professor Kim's article without charge.

New Study Released

New Report Examines Changing Worship Practices in the United States

Congregations that have adopted innovative worship and contemporary worship styles are significantly more likely to have grown in the last five years. These are among the findings in a new Faith Communities Today 2010 report titled “FACTs on Worship.” FACT is releasing this latest analysis to help congregations understand how worship practices can affect their vitality and inspire their members.
